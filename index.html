About Me

I am a Computational Biologist with extensive wet-lab expertise, working for over 8 years in the characterization of novel proteins/enzymes and studying structure-function interconnections during evolution. I specialize in integrating wet-lab and computational methods.

Currently, I am focusing on Protein Design, developing custom workflows that integrate AI- and physics-based methods to generate binders for folded and unfolded epitopes of medically relevant biomarkers.

Current Main Project

AI-Driven Design of CDKL5 Binders with Minimal Screening

I am developing a computational pipeline to design binders for studying the biology of challenging biomarkers like CDKL5 kinase, poorly immunogenic/druggable, as different isoforms and closely related paralogue kinases exist. In this project we need to target specific epitopes, both in folded and unfolded epitopes, finding binders that are also developable for cell-based studies.

The key challenge to address is going to be achieving both binding and developability success with minimal experimental screening (<15 characterized designs). This will be done by efficiently leveraging multiple AI-based and physics-based agents over several thousands of generated candidates. This will allow to bypass the severe high-throughput experimental bottleneck that exist for targeting this biomarker.
